We must change constitution and Raila must be President in 2022 - Babu Owino

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Saturday, June 15, 2024

- Babu said referendum will address electoral injustice in the country

- The Embakasi East MP noted reforms will also save the country from perennial chaos witnessed every electioneering year

- He, however, insisted Raila must be president in 2022

Embakasi East MP Babu Owino has asked Kenyans to prepare for a Raila Odinga presidency in 2022 after the referendum.

The youthful politician said it was high time the constitution was change to address electoral injustices in the country and pave way for a new leadership of Raila.

Through his Twitter handle on Friday, August 9, the lawmaker asked Kenyans to turn out in large numbers and support the referendum and save the country from perennial political upheavals every electioneering period.

"We must change the constitution and Raila must be the president in 2022. Kenyans must prepare for a Referendum to avoid loss of lives and destruction of properties after elections," he tweeted

Babu is Raila's ardent supporter and has been boldly taking on those opposed to the former premier's political advancements.

While the Opposition supremo has not declared his interest for the top seat in 2022, his close allies like Babu believe he is the man to watch in the coming General Election.

Similar utterances were made by Siaya Senator James Orengo, another Raila confidant, who asked Kenyans to prepare for the ODM leader's candidature in 2022.

East Africa Legislative Assembly (EALA) member, Oburu Odinga, Raila's elder brother, has also hinted at Opposition chief's presidential bid in 2022.

A section of leaders allied to Deputy President William Ruto who is also eyeing succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta, have been accusing Raila of pushing for referendum to create a political space for himself.

Babu's sentiments ignited heated a social media engagement with a section of netizens using his words to relate Raila's hidden agenda in the famous handshake deal with Uhuru.

The Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) which has been moving around the country to collect views of Kenyans on the subject is set to present its report to Uhuru and Raila in September.

From the detailed report, questions will be framed and put to Kenyans decide if they support the proposed draft or not.

While no specific time frame has been outlined for the exercise, Raila has been on record saying referendum will be held before the end of the year.
