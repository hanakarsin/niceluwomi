Kenyans Uncomfortable Keeping Family Members As Social Media Friends, TUKO.co.ke Poll Shows

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, April 9, 2024

- To have family members as friends on social media or to block them out completely?

- That is the dilemma that many people have these days considering the internet exists as space for people to express themselves

- A survey by TUKO.co.ke shows that most people are not comfortable letting family members into this space

Social media is generally a space where individuals get to express themselves behind a keyboard.

In most instances, people post and share content that ranges from intellectual to naughty to controversial.

Sometimes, however, this freedom is curtailed when the individual has close members of his or her family in the friendship list because they have to censor content.

This feeling of being under constant watch from people who often get offended by posts makes many choose to block out family members and parents from their sites.

Titus M Joshua:

"I joined social media to interact with new friends, not family members. If it is a matter of family I can host a family gathering."

Millicent Manyalla

"My accounts have walls of Jericho around them, I do not want CCTV cameras on my back. I can't even use my memes in peace, every time I post or comment they find something wrong with it."

Another school of thought does not support blocking, arguing that it is good to have members of the family as social media friends because they keep one in check.

James Wachira

"You don't need to block. You need to be careful with what you post or like. They too must be careful. Posting trash will create shame and conflict."

Overall, the bigger majority opines that letting family members into one's social media space has the potential to create loggerheads with family members.
