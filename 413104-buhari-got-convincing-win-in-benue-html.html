Buhari Wins Convincingly In Benue

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Thursday, August 22, 2024

General Muhammadu Buhari, the All Progressives Congress presidential candidate, has at last beaten the incumbent President Goodluck Jonathan in the presidential poll outcome in Benue state.

Buhari received a clear victory with over 70,000 extra votes after the long-awaited results for Makurdi, the state capital, came in around 2am on Tuesday, March 31.

The APC presidential candidate scored 38,685 votes in Makurdi to bring his overall total votes in the 23 local government areas of the state to 373,961.

The current head of the country polled 19,739 votes in Makurdi to bring his total vote haul to 303,737.

The two presidential aspirants had been in a head-to-head race in the state since Sunday when‎ results of the poll started trickling in.

Jonathan led when the results of the first five local governments were announced and remained in the lead till around 11am on Monday.

However, Buhari later overcame him and continued to lead slightly till he won convincingly on Tuesday morning.

In the results declared by the presidential election collation officer in the state, Prof. Hayward Mafuyai‎, in Makurdi, Jonathan won in 14 local governments, while Buhari took the remaining nine.

While the president won more local governments, Buhari polled higher votes in the nine he won.

Jonathan won in Ogbadibo, Ukum, Agatu, Ado, Okpokwu, Ohimini, Guma, Otukpo, Apa, Gwer-West, Oju, Obi, Katsina-Ala and Logo.

On the other hand, Buhari won in Tarka, Gboko, Gwer-East, Ushongo, Konshi‎sha, Makurdi, Vandeikya, Buruku and Kwande.
