Eid al-Adha is one of the most anticipated and holiest Muslim holidays. It is regarded as the biggest celebration after Hajj. It commemorates the height of the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca. The word Eid is an Arabic term that means recurring celebration, while Eid al-Adha translates to Feast of the Sacrifice. Eid al-Adha is a holiday that commemorates the time Prophet Ibrahim wanted to sacrifice his only son as an act of his faith in God. Best Eid al-Adha Mubarak messages and wishes

What is the Eid al-Adha story?

Eid al-Adha is one of the two holidays celebrated each year by Muslim believers. The festival honours the willingness of Ibrahim to sacrifice his only son to show his devotion and obedience to God's command. Before Ibrahim sacrificed his son, God provided a ram to be sacrificed instead.

What do we say when someone says Eid Mubarak?

If someone says Eid Mubarak to you, it is polite to respond by saying Khair Mubarak, which wishes goodwill on the person who greeted you.

Is Eid al-Adha a holiday in Kenya?

Eid al-Adha is an optional holiday in Kenya. Employment and holiday laws in the country allow employees to choose a limited number of holidays from a list of optional holidays.

What is the difference between Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha?

Eid Al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan and is characterised by feasting, gift-giving, and acts of charity. On the other hand, Eid Al-Adha coincides with the culmination of the Hajj pilgrimage and focuses on sacrifice, reflection, and communal prayers.
