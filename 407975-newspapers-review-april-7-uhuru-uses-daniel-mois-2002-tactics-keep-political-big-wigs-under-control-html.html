Newspapers Review for April 7: Uhuru Uses Daniel Moi's 2002 Tactics to Keep Political Big Wigs Under

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Monday, June 10, 2024

The papers on Wednesday, April 7, reported on the uproar among Kenyans protesting the International Monetary Funds (IMF) loan to the Jubilee government.

The dailies also reported on ongoing political realignments as key politicians meet to form yet another alliance ahead of the 2022 elections.

1. Taifa Leo

This daily reports on President Uhuru Kenyatta's political strategy which is synonymous with late president Daniel Moi's tactic in the months leading to the end of his tenure in 2002.

Currently, Uhuru has managed to keep all the major political big wigs at bay as they all anticipate his endorsement to run for the presidency in 2022.

According to political analyst Muriithi Mutiga, Uhuru's game of chess could prove deadly if he betrays politicians such as ODM leader Raila Odinga who could join forces with Deputy President William Ruto and trounce the presidency.

The analyst said just as Moi disappointed his close associates and endorsed Uhuru, a then political novice, and who lost the election to Narc's Mwai Kibaki, the same fate could befall the Jubilee Party leader.

2. Daily Nation

According to Daily Nation, President Uhuru Kenyatta has defended stringent COVID-19 protocols for churches around the country amidst an uproar from religious leaders.

The president said the COVID-19 rules were extremely necessary if Kenya was determined to win the war against the deadly virus.

Uhuru urged churches to instead focus on utilising social media and community radios to spread a word of hope during these tumultuous times.

This came in the wake of disquiet from a section of religious leaders who said the laid out COVID-19 rules were affecting church attendance and a dip in revenue.

3. The Standard

According to the Standard, another political alliance could be in the making after key political leaders held a six-hour meeting at a Nairobi hotel on Tuesday, April 6.

The high-profile meeting brought together Mukhisa Kituyi, governors Alfred Mutua (Machakos), Charity Ngilu (Kitui), Anyang' Nyong'o (Kisumu), Kivutha Kibwana (Makueni), Mohamed Kuti (Isiolo), Amason Kingi (Kilifi) and Siaya senator James Orengo.

The meeting set tongues wagging with rife speculation that yet another political marriage could be in the offing.

A source revealed more meeting of such calibre will happen in the coming weeks and months as the politicians seek to solidify their political alliance.

4. People Daily

Physical sittings in the Nairobi county assembly have been suspended indefinitely due to a spike in COVID-19 infections, People Daily reports.

Leader of Majority Abdi Guyo said many ward reps had contracted the virus including himself.

Speaker Benson Mutura said over 50 MCAs had contracted COVID-19 with 35 of them recovering. He said four were hospitalised with two being on oxygen therapy.

5. The Star

According to this newspaper, four administrators from Homa Bay have been interdicted for failure to enforce COVID-19 rules in their area of jurisdiction.

Rachuonyo North deputy county commissioner Aaron Cheruiyot Koros and assistant county commissioner East Karachuonyo Abigail Bett were among those punished.
