Lulu: 8-year-old dog inherits KSh 500 million from deceased owner

- The owner died at 84 Thewith no wife to whom he could bequeath his massive holdings

One Tennessee man went above and beyond to ensure his beloved dog would be taking care of after his death.

Bill Dorris died in 2020 but he had left a small fortune for his 8-year-old dog Lulu in his will which stipulated that upon his death, $5 million (KS 500 million) would be transferred to a trust for his border collie.

According to a report by People, te do is now under the care of Bills friend Martha Burton who in addition to being Lulu's current caretaker, she spent time with the canine whenever her previous owner was out of town on business.

"He always left the dog for me to take care of. She's a good girl," Burton added, saying admiringly of the pooch.

Despite the vast sum of money te do as inherited, Lulu is not pawing off with a briefcase of cash.

Per the will, Burton has been named as Lulu's official caretaker and any "reasonable" costs associated with the dog's care will need to be submitted before being reimbursed.

Although it was not specified what will happen to the remainder of the trust after Lulu's death, Vanity Fair previously reported that any unused money typically gets left to an individual or charity.

As for what's next for Lulu, Burton said she's looking forward to making sure the dog is well-taken care of and seeing how close the pair can get to use up the money.

This is not the first time a pet owner has bequeathed a fortune onto a lucky dog.

In 2015, a Queens woman rewrote her will to leave her Maltese a million-dollar fortune consisting of jewellery, a trust fund and vacation home.
