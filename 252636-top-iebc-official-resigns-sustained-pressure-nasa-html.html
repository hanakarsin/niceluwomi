Top IEBC official resigns amid sustained pressure from NASA

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Wednesday, March 13, 2024

- IEBC Legal Affairs officer Praxedes Tororey has succumbed to sustained pressure from NASA and resigned from her post

- Tororey was accused by NASA of failing to advise IEBC correctly on the election laws and procedures that would lead to a clean election

- NASA has been demanding the removal of Tororey and 11 others over the bungled August 8 election

- Her resignation comes after the Supreme Court found that the commission failed to follow the right procedures in conducting elections

The sustained pressure from the National Super Alliance (NASA) coalition has seen one of the IEBC officials implicated tender her resignation letter.

Praxedes Tororey, the IEBC legal affairs officer, has resigned from her post just two days after the Supreme Court delivered its full judgement on the nullified election.

TUKO.co.ke understands Tororey left her post on the morning of Friday, September 22, despite the Supreme Court failing to point fingers at individuals over the mess.

Tororey was one of the 12 officials of the electoral body that the opposition named and demanded their removal for allegedly bungling the election.

NASA accused her of failing to advise the commission appropriately on the electoral laws and the procedures that would ensure a clean election.

Others implicated include CEO Ezra Chiloba whom NASA has named as the architect of the mess.

Tororey's resignation comes hours to a meeting between NASA and the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Keriako Tobiko at the Hotel Intercontinental where they are expected to present their case against IEBC.

NASA wants Tobiko to investigate and charge the officials who helped rig the election.
