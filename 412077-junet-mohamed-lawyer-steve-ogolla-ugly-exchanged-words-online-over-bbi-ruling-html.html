Junet Mohamed, Lawyer Steve Ogolla Lock Horns over High Court Ruling on BBI

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Wednesday, June 12, 2024

- Suna East MP Junet Mohamed and lawyer Steve Ogolla shapely differed over the landmark ruling that stopped BBI reggae

- Ogolla claimed Junet did not understand the ruling delivered by the court, something that did not go down well with the lawmaker

- The differences between the two played out shortly after the BBI secretariat issued a press statement disagreeing with the ruling, and vowing to continue with the process

Suna East MP Junet Mohamed and constitutional lawyer Steve Ogolla have sharply differed over the landmark ruling delivered by a five-judge bech regarding the constitutionality of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) process.

On Thursday, May 13, the high court declared the bill, whose process commenced three years ago, unconstitutional, null and void.

The court also declared the BBI secretariat, which Junet co-chairs, as an illegal entity that should not act on behalf of Kenyans.

The ruling did not go down well with promoters of the bill who held a press conference on Friday, May 14, to express their dissatisfaction.

The Suna East lawmaker who read the statement claimed the ruling only disqualified the president from driving the BBI process, indicating the secretariat would continue with its work normally.

The National Assembly minority leader also told the press promoters of the bill were in the process of organising a team of lawyers to challenge the ruling before the Court of Appeal.

Shortly after the press, Ogolla took to his Twitter handle and suggested Junet did not understand the ruling of the court.

"I watched an interview when Junet was talking about the Hight Court ruling on BBI, and three things emerged about his knowledge on the ruling," Ogolla wrote.

The lawyer claimed that the MP had not read the BBI judgement, and could comprehend the judgment.

In response, Junet told the lawyer that he might not comprehend the BBI ruling but Ogolla had done "incomprehensible" things before.

Earlier, TUKO.ke.co reported about the fury expressed by BBI secretariat and plans to challenge the ruling next week.

They termed the anti-BBI judgement as judicial activism.

"Consequently, we are setting up a legal team to look into and appeal the ruling. In the meantime, we assure all Kenyans who believe in the BBI not to give up. It's not over yet; we still believe a new Kenya is possible. We will fight to the bitter end for that dream to be realised," the secretariat stated in a statement read by Junet.
