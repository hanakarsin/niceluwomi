Components of romantic messages

In an interview with Legit.ng, Bose Fawehinmi, the Founder and Lead Coach at Marriage Matters International, outlined the essential components of effective romantic messages and how people can make them memorable for their partners. The components are:

  • It should communicate your feelings about your partner or what they mean to you.
  • It should describe the relationship or the bond you share. Bose explained:
    • You wouldn't just want to write a quote and make it so abstract that your partner is not being referred to; you are not being referred to; your love and your relationship are not being referred to.You are quoting something from the universe that has nothing to do with you. You cannot just say, 'Oh, see how bright the sun is, full stop.' If the sun is bright, what does that do with me? But if you say, 'See how the sun is so bright, and that's how you brighten my world,' you have connected me to the sun's brightness so that I can appreciate that.

    3. It should be meaningful and allow your partner to see themselves in your words. She emphasized:

    Before you say words, make sure that they are meaningful that your partner can practically see themselves in what you are saying or see a description of the union you guys share or something about you guys there. So when you are lifting things from the Internet, be careful.There may be a quote you like, but the quote probably refers to a broken heart, a shattered love, or a love that no longer exists. Just because you like it does not mean you should use it. If your partner interprets the meaning as something they don't like, it can cause issues.You don't want to send messages that have dual meanings. Make sure that what they say, even with a negative connotation, still represents what you guys are going through.
