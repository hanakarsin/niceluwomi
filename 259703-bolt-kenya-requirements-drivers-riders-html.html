Bolt Kenya requirements for drivers and riders: Complete guide

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Tuesday, June 4, 2024

As Taxify rebrands to Bolt with the intention of getting its roots deeper in the Kenyan market, several drivers who have expressed an interest in joining the platform are uncertain about the requirements. Fortunately, Bolt Kenya requirements and sign up process is quite easy to hack.

Major towns in Kenya have greatly developed since the launch of the 2010 Constitution, which brought about devolution. While job opportunities expand across the country following this initiative, the resultant economic effect and new administrative units have necessitated the introduction of convenient and affordable means of transport. Bolt has taken full advantage of this noble opportunity to meet the huge demand.

Bolt is a universal transportation service that uses the same online model like Uber or Little Cab. People can use the Bolt app to ask for a taxi or a personal driver through their cell phones wherever they are. While it was previously available only in major towns such as Nairobi and Mombasa, Bolt, formerly Taxify, is hoping to boost its success by expanding its geographical reach to other towns such as Thika, Kisumu, and Kakamega.

Bolt manages fleets and current taxi organization drivers. To become a Bolt driver, you ought to work with an organization or association, affiliation or an armada. Thanks to the lower commission rates, the drivers have a chance to earn more. You must meet the accompanying fundamental Bolt Kenya requirements to be accepted as a Bolt driver.

Bolt Kenya requirements for drivers

If you want to be considered as a Bolt driveryou must present the following documents and meet certain conditions.

  • Provide a clear picture of the vehicle.
  • Passport-size photo
  • Have a legitimate National ID or Passport
  • An Android or iPhone Smartphone with GPS
  • Police Clearance Certificate
  • PSV insurance sticker
  • NTSA report/sticker
  • Driver’s normal driving license
  • Driver’s PSV driving license
  • The vehicle's Logbook

Bolt transport platform focuses on ensuring faster, easier, and more reliable urban travel in and outside Kenya. Being an international organization, it is crucial to adhere to Bolt Kenya driver requirements to protect its reputation.

Cars accepted by Bolt Kenya

There are specific requirements for cars accepted by Bolt Kenya that you should know before registering your vehicle on the platform. The vehicle year of manufacture must be:

  • From 2009 onwards in Nairobi
  • From 2008 onwards in Mount Kenya area, Kakamega, and Kisumu
  • From 2007 onwards in Mombasa

Bolt Kenya also accepts Boda Boda drivers in case you want to operate your motorcycle on this platform.

How do I become a Bolt driver in Kenya?

Subsequent to providing the above records and ensuring that you meet all the stated requirements, you must also sign up on their platform to create a driver account. You will be required to upload all the necessary documents during the Bolt sign up process. Finally, upon successful affirmation as a driver, you will need to visit their office in Nairobi for training at Azure Towers, 2nd Floor, Lantana Road, Westlands on any weekday at 11:00 am.

Can I use multiple cars with one bolt account?

If you have more than one car then you have to create a fleet owner account. Next, log in to the driver portal and add the new vehicle. It will take 24 hours for the new car application to be verified after which you can easily switch from one driver profile to another depending on which vehicle you want to work with at the start of the day.

How long does it take to become a Bolt driver?

Submitting your data takes approximately 5 minutes. After that, you will get a 30-minute training in their office. In a few urban areas, the company will acknowledge your application remotely once you provide them with all the vital reports. Once confirmed, your account will be activated. You can then download the Bolt app and officially become a Bolt Driver.

The best thing about Bolt Kenya careers is that the company cares for its drivers like it does its customers. And you will only have to pay a small percentage as commission for the company.

How can I contact Bolt?

It is more convenient to search for Bolt Kenya contacts at your nearest city using Bolt App. You can also reach them in Kenya by visiting their office in Nairobi located in Azure Towers, 2nd Floor, Lantana Road in Westlands any day of the week from 10 AM to 4 PM except on Friday where it is closed at 2 PM.

Bolt customer care number Kenya

Although Bolt customer care number Kenya is not available online, you can easily contact them with just a click of a button once you have downloaded the app.
