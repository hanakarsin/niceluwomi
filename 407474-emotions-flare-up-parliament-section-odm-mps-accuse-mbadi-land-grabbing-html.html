Emotions Flare Up in Parliament as Section of ODM MPs Accuse Mbadi of Land-grabbing

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, May 7, 2024

- Nyatike MP Tom Odege had sought clarification from the Intergovernmental Committee on Administration and National Security on the causes of the increased border conflicts in the Utati area that sits along with Migori and Homa Bay counties

- But Mbadi who doubles as the minority leader in the National Assembly termed the issue at hand as very emotive saying that it should be treated with a lot of care and soberness

- Ali Fatuma Gedi, Vice-Chair, Administrative, and National Security moved to assure the House that the committee will respond to the statement made by Odege within two weeks

Suba Member of Parliament John Mbadi found himself on the receiving end after his colleagues from the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) accused him of being a land grabber.

The drama started after Nyatike MP Tom Odege moved to the floor of the House to seek clarification from the Intergovernmental Committee on Administration and National Security on the causes of the increased border conflicts in the Utati area that sits along Migori and Homa Bay counties.

Odege alleged that there have been persistent conflicts and fights between the residents of the Utati area and along the borders of Migori and Homa Bay counties.

"The latest conflict was on Friday, January 8, 2021, and tension has continued to date which has greatly affected the market days in the Utati area leading to loss of livelihoods," Odege said.

He further said that there have been conflicts in the past within the border of Uriri and Nyatike constituencies at the Kimulich area.

"It is against this backdrop that I seek a statement from the Chairperson of the Intergovernmental Committee on Administration and National Security on the causes of the increased border conflicts in Utati area along Migori and Homa Bay counties," said Odege.

Odege questioned the Committee on the measures the Ministry of Internal Security had taken to clear demarcate Migori boundaries at the Utati sub-location.

"Could the ministry consider having a police post in the Kimulich area and what plans has the ministry put in place for long-term and sustainable peace in this area?" he quizzed even as he termed Mbadi as a beneficiary of Nyatike land and accused him of being a land grabber.

In a quick rejoinder, Mbadi who doubles as the minority leader in the National Assembly termed the issue at hand as very emotive saying that it should be treated with a lot of care and soberness.

"The MP of Nyatike claimed while finishing his statement that I am a beneficiary of the land of Nyatike people. I want to say that there is no conflict around that area. The MP of Nyatike, comes from about 20kms to 25kms away from Utati. My home is 3kms to Utati. So he cannot claim that he knows what is happening in Utati more than I do," explained Mbadi.

He claimed that the people of Karungu and Gwassi have lived harmoniously over the years.

"We intermarry and do other socio-economic activities together. Utati is where there is a cattle market and that cattle market is right inside my constituency and nobody can take it away. That land belongs to Gwassi and there is nothing to even investigate," he added.

He rejected any plans by the committee to visit the disputed area with a view of investigating the cause of the conflict.

"The committee has enough work to do not to go and look into issues that amount to ego. The Nyatike MP does not respect my constituency. There are no problems between Suba and Karungu people. If he is looking for votes then he should not use Utati to do so. He has been my friend but he is now stepping on a live wire," Mbadi further said.

Suna East MP Junet Mohamed sarcastically claimed that Mbadi's constituency lacks a shopping centre as he is forced to do his shopping in the neighboring Migori County.

"One thing I agree with my party leader is that in his constituency, where he goes shopping there is no single shopping centre so he has to go all the way to Nyatike. Many times I have visited him I have waited for lunch for four hours. When I ask him he tells me that the food is coming from Karungu and so I should be patient, the food is on the bicycle coming," said the MP amid laughter from members.

Junet said that the issue raised by the Nyatike MP was very important and that the Committee needs to move with speed and look into it.

"Mbadi is trying to push the boundary inside every day. On behalf of Migori County, our land has been grabbed by Homa Bay County and we want GSU deployed there to help recover it," said Junet.

Migori County MP Pamela Awour said that she had on several occasions hosted delegations from Utati at her home who come to look for help over the matter.

"So if the MP for Suba is alleging that Utati is in his constituency then he needs to explain further to this House why he is not taking care of his own people. Utati is in Karungu which is part of Nyatike and the big part of the land we are talking about is in Migori County," said the Woman MP.

Suba North MP Millie Odhiambo said that she had experienced a similar situation with Mbadi in the first parliament where she was the one who was considered an aggressor yet she was the one who had raised the particular matter.

"I want to say that these are very emotive issues that we should not be playing with on the floor of the House. As members, we can make it as a joke and laugh at it here but people who are listening on the ground don’t take these issues lightly," Millie said.

She cautioned that the matter could easily degenerate into a wall between Nyatike and Suba South, escalating into a war between Homa Bay and Migori Counties.

"We should not take advantage of Baba’s (Raila Odinga) health to embarrass the ODM party in this House. As a party, we should not clean our dirty linen in public," said Millie.

Uriri MP Mark Nyamita said that about a year ago, they have had to deploy police officers at the porous border to stay there for almost a week keeping the peace.

"There has been an issue between Uriri and Nyatike however, I want to challenge my neighbor from Nyatike that we do find time and hold a public engagement there to iron out the issues that are there," he said.

He said that if he goes on his own then those issues are not able to be sorted but expressed confidence that as local leaders they can sit amongst themselves and iron out their differences.

Nyamita said that the same would apply between the border of Nyatike and Suba South.

"This is a small matter that can be addressed if we have a sit down at the centre of the conflict. Even as we welcome the committee to come and do their investigations, as local leaders we can sit down and solve these issues amicably," Nyamita added.

Ali Fatuma Gedi, Vice-Chair, Administrative, and National Security moved to assure the House that the committee will respond to the statement within two weeks.

"Since it is also the right of the Honourable member to have a response to his statement. I want to assure the members and also the legislators from Migori and Homa Bay that we will do justice to this," said Gedi.
