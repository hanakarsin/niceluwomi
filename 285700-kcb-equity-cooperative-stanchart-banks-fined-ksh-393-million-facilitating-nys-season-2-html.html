KCB, Equity, Cooperative and StanChart banks fined KSh 393 million for facilitating NYS season 2

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Sunday, April 7, 2024

- CBK said the banks failed to report suspicious transactions to the Financial Reporting Center (FRC)

- The banks were used by NYS fraud suspects who pilfered at least KSh 9 billion from the institution

- KCB is set to pay the heftiest fine of KSh 149. 5 million for transacting KSh 639 million from NYS

- Cooperative bank will pay the least amount of KSh 20 million for receiving KSh 263 million from NYS

The Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) on Wednesday, September 12, fined five top banks to a tune of KSh 393 million for facilitating the second National Youth Service (NYS) scam.

According to CBK, the five lenders were used by people who were suspected of transacting fraudulently acquired money from NYS.

TUKO.co.ke reported the second scam to befall the NYS was exposed on May 2018 and it saw at least KSh 9 billion in taxpayers money pilfered by fraudulent officials and ghost tenders.

This resulted to at least 40 suspects being arraigned, among them former Principal Secretary Lillian Omolo and former NYS Director General Richard Ndubai.

The CBK fined KCB, Equity, Standard Chartered, Cooperative and Diamond Trust (DTB) Banks.

The regulator's investigations revealed the financial institutions were involved in transacting money stolen from the NYS.

According to a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke, KCB will pay the highest fine of KSh 149 million while Equity bank comes second with a penalty of KSh 89.5 million.

Standard Chartered Bank was penalised KSh 77.5 million, Diamond Trust bank KSh 56 million while Cooperative Bank was asked to pay the least amount at KSh 20 million.

The regulator revealed over KSh 3.6 billion was transacted through the five banks where Standard Chartered Bank handled the highest amount of KSh 1.6 billion.

Equity handled KSh 886 million, KCB KSh 639 million while DTB handled KSh 162 million of the loot.

“CBK has discussed the detailed findings with Boards of Directors and Senior Management of each of the banks. Each has expressed their strong commitment to be fully compliant on all aspects of the law, and addressing the identified lapses through time-bound Action Plans,” reads the statement from CBK.

The CBK said the financial institutions were subject to the penalties as they failed to report huge money transfers and also for failing to execute customer due diligence.

The other fault, CBK said, was that they (the 5 banks) had lapses in reporting Suspicious Transactions (STR's) to the Financial Reporting Center (FRC)

“The main objective of the investigations was to examine the operations of the NYS-related bank accounts and transactions, and in each instance assess the bank’s compliance with the requirements of Kenya’s Anti-Money Laundering/Combating Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) laws and regulations,” said CBK.
