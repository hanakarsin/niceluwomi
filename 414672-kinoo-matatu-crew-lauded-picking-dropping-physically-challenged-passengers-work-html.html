Kinoo Matatu Crew Lauded for Picking and Dropping Physically Challenged Passengers from Work

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Saturday, February 3, 2024

- Anthony Gikonyo and his conductor help physically challenged people to get home from work without any difficulties

- The matatu does not charge the passengers extra costs for easing their travel and does this every day of work

- The driver said he saw how they struggled to get to the stage and thought he would help

A matatu crew from Kinoo, Kiambu county is showing an immense level of kindness by its efforts to help the physically challenged passengers live an easy life.

In a story covered by NTV, driver Anthony Gikonyo and his conductor have been picking and dropping people living with disabilities from the Association for the Physically Disabled of Kenya (APDK) offices in Westlands.

The crew plying the Kangemi - Westlands route, does not overcharge their special customers and makes two trips to and from the offices every day to ensure they serve everyone.

The driver said that his kind act started two years ago when he noticed the physically disabled having a problem getting to the matatu stage and to make it worse, it was raining.

"One day I met them at the stage and I saw how they struggled with the rain and I sympathised with them. I told them 'from tomorrow I will be ferrying you,'" he said.

One of her passengers, Magdalene Mbugua, said she calls Anthony as soon as she's out of her house and the driver always promises to wait for her, and they pay the same as other passengers.

"He has not overcharged us for the trips. He's not a person who is looking to gain out of this but he has a good heart," Mbugua said.

Anthony said he hopes to be a good example to his kids who will hopefully have the heart to help others when they grow up.

The matatu industry is known for its 'speedy' kind of lifestyle with no time for people with slow reactions, but for Anthony's crew, caring comes first.

Their efforts were lauded by many on social media with some noting they deserved state recognition for their work.

Feed thy passengers

In another act of kindness, TUKO.co.ke recently reported about a Mombasa matatu crew that decided to feed their passengers with fast food meals on their first day at work.

In many Nairobi routes, new Nganyas (sheng for matatu) entertain its passengers with free rides to and from town as a way of showing off what they have in store, but for this one- it was different.

In a video shared by Matwana Culture on Instagram, one could spot KFC food packages well placed on several seats with others empty as the owners followed the strict COVID-19 guidelines.
