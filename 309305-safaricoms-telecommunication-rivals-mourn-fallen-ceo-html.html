Bob Collymore: Safaricom's telecommunication competitors mourn fallen CEO

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Sunday, March 31, 2024

- Telkom and Airtel sent their condolences messages through Twitter

- The companies hailed the Guyanese national for being an astute leader

- Kenyans lauded the two companies for putting competition aside and joining Safaricom in mourning

Telecommunication companies Airtel and Telkom have joined millions of Kenyans in mourning the late Safaricom CEO Bob Collymore.

Just like most Kenyans, the two competing telecom companies expressed their shock following the sudden demise of the able Guyana-born CEO.

Airtel, in a tweet said the telecommunication industry had lost one of the best minds in the business.

The company noted it will stand with its competitor during this trying time.

''It is with great sadness that we have received the news of the passing on of Safaricom PLC CEO Bob Collymore. As a telecommunication industry, we have lost an astute businessman who played a leading role in development and enhancement of the industry. We would like to express our sincerest condolences to his family, staff at Safaricom PLC, friends and those he interacted with,'' Airtel tweeted.

On its part, Telkom celebrated the late Collymore as a steadfast and visionary leader.

''No words can adequately express my sadness upon hearing the news nor the gratitude I have for having had the opportunity to have had him as an industry colleague,'' Telkom CEO Mugo Kibati said in an official statement from the company.

The move by the two Safaricom competitors was lauded by countless Kenyans who congratulated them for putting their competition aside and joining hands in mourning.

Bob died on Monday, July 1, morning at his Kitusuru home.

The departed CEO had been battling Leukemia for months and was in 2017 forced to take a year-long medical leave to seek treatment in the United Kingdom (UK).
