Kevin Omwenga: Controversial businessman was to travel to Dubai for KSh 100M payment

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Sunday, February 11, 2024

- Controversial businessman Kevin Omwenga was shot dead at his house in Galana Suites on Friday, August 21

- Prior to his death, Omwenga was set to travel to Dubai with his neighbour and friend identified as Jamal

- It was reported that they were to travel together to collect KSh 100 million payment in a unknown business deal

- The deceased was laid to rest at his village home in Kitutu Chache on Saturday, August 29

New details have emerged to the effect that controversial businessman Kevin Omwenga was set to travel to Dubai for KSh 100 million payment in a business deal whose details are scanty.

Omwenga was to travel to the Arab country in the company of his friend and neighbour only identified as Jamal.

In a report by Citizen TV, detectives are seeking to establish if the main suspects Chris OBure and Robert Bodo were part of the business deal in what detectives suspect may have led to the death of Omwenga.

The Dubai trip failed to materialise after the overnight millionaire met his death at his apartment in Galana Suites, Kilimani estate on Friday, August 21.

Also, messages retrieved from the deceased's phone showed he had communicated to his shooter Bodo who thanked him for unknown assistance that Omwenga had offered him.

“You’re a true brother you helped me” wrote Bodo to Omwenga, to which the deceased replied “welcome bro”.

Omwenga was laid to rest on Saturday, August 29, at his rural home in Kitutu Chache, Kisii county, with a number of high-profile controversial city businessmen attending the event.

Among those who attended included renowned Zaheer Jhanda and Don Bosco who at one point were mentioned in a fake gold scam involving Saudi Arabia's royal family.

Another notable businessman who attended the event and has been surrounded with controversies was former Starehe MP aspirant Steve Mbogo.

They all eulogised the 28-year-old man as hardworking and urged the police to expedite investigations so that the family can get justice.

Already, police have recorded statement from those who were in the house on the day Omwenga was shot dead including a mysterious woman of Tanzanian origin who was captured on CCTV entering the house.

In her statement, the woman told the police she was just getting to know the deceased and she knew so little about his life.

Businessman Obure and his bodyguard Bodo are still in custody pending conclusion of investigations into the matter.
