Early life

Emmanuel Kipkirui Korir is a Kenyan professional athlete born on June 15, 1995, in a village near Iten, Kenya. The athlete had a normal rural upbringing and attended Kimuron Secondary School in Iten, Kenya. Emmanuel and David Rudisha attended the same high school.

Korir would train at the Bro Colm O'Connell Iten track and field production line as a young man. He is one of the individuals mentored by an Irish missionary who had a passion for developing athletic talent.

Kipkirui lives a private life, and the details of his upbringing have remained a mystery. His parents are also not known, although the athlete dedicated his gold medal to his father, who he said has been one of the greatest supporters in his career. His mother and siblings are also not in the limelight.

Career

Emmanuel grew up primarily as a 400m runner. Emmanuel Korir's 400m championships include first place in El Paso Conference USA Ch, El Paso, Texas. While studying here, Korir broke the ''school and meet'' record in the 400m at the UTEP Invitational setting in a time of 44.67 in 2017.

Later that year, he scored 12.5 points at the C-USA Championships, winning gold in the 400m with a time of 44.53. He was also part of the 4x400m relay team, which garnered a silver medal with a time of 3:06.94.

In 2016, a Kenyan coach and former athlete, Paul Ereng, recruited him to study at The University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP), and Korir began running the 800 m. Paul Ereng was the winner of the 800 metres at the 1988 Summer Olympics.

Ereng invested in Emmanuel Korir's training, which helped him develop stamina and endurance by taking long-distance and cross-country runs. In 2017, the athlete shifted focus to the 800m race. The following year, Emmanuel worked to build his strength by regularly attending the gym.

Emmanuel Korir in 800m

Korir left UTEP in 2017 to take up a professional running contract with American multinational footwear company Nike Inc. In January 2017, Emmanuel made history when he won the 600m indoor world record in Albuquerque, New Mexico, at the Cherry and Silver Invitational Friday night. His running time was an impressive 1:14.97.

The same year, Korir opened the season at the Jim Buss Invitational, running in the 800m clocking a time of 1:47.19 to take first place. He also ran the anchor leg of the 4x400m relay helping the team finish in second place.

Kipkirui also contested in the Brutus Hamilton Challenge 800m and bagged the gold medal. He clocked 1:43.73. Unknown to many, Emmanuel is one of three athletes in the world to run a sub-45 second 400m and a sub-1:44 in the 800m in 2018.

Kipkirui also qualified to the (NCAA National Collegiate Athletic Association) West Region Preliminary in the 800m, winning gold in the first of two heats with a time of 1:45.88. He clenched his second national title in the 800m at the NCAA Championships, running a time of 1:45.03, making him the first Miner to capture back-to-back national championships in the same event.

Kipkirui's diamond league performance has also been impressive, with six meeting wins to his name between 2018 and 2021. The wins have taken place in venues like Monaco, Birmingham, Brussels and London.
