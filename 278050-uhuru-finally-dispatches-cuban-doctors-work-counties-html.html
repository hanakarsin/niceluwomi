Uhuru finally dispatches Cuban doctors to work in counties

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Wednesday, July 17, 2024

- Uhuru said the move would greatly improve local capacity urging Kenyans to support the foreign doctors

- The court allowed the Cuba doctors to work in Kenya despite a protest from local medics

- Uhuru also launched a campaign to immunize 400,000 children in 100 days

President Uhuru Kenyatta on Wednesday, June 27. urged Kenyans to support Cuban doctors as they officially embarked on their services across 42 counties in the country.

The Head of State noted this as he formally released the 100 Cuban medics to provide their highly anticipated services to members of the public at the county level.

The president expressed joy as he dispatched the specialists to deliver the much needed medical services while urging Kenyans to offer the foreign medics support.

"Delighted to formally send-off the 100 Cuban doctors specializing in cardiology, nephrology and neurosurgery, amongst other specializations to Counties to deliver the much needed services to the people of Kenya. Let us embrace and support them," Uhuru said .

Uhuru further stated the plan which also involved sending 50 Kenyan physicians to Cuba for specialised training would greatly improve local capacity.

"The bilateral agreement between Kenya and Cuba will therefore enhance health collaboration in specialised service provision and also build capacity of our local doctors," he said.

The president was speaking at the launch of a campaign which targets to immunize 400,000 children in 100 days.

This comes barely a week after Kenyan doctors were dealt a huge blow following the ruling by the Labour Relation Court to approve the government's move to deploy the Cuban doctors citing urgent need to provide Kenyans with medical services.

Local medics had vehemently objected the proposition demanding that the government first hires 1,200 local doctors who were unemployed.

However, in his verdict, Labour Court judge Nzioka Makau stated there was no proof that there were unemployed specialist medics as earlier claimed by Kenyan doctors through their union, Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentist Union (KMPDU).
