Murang'a police impound fake currency worth KSh 909K, arrest 3 suspects

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Friday, June 14, 2024

- One of the suspect, Stephen Murigi, was arrested at Kabati Police Station where he had been summoned by the OCS over another case

- Two other suspects, Muthoni and Njoroge, were arrested at Kaburugi trading centre in Ng'araria after they tried to con an M-Pesa agent

Police in Murang'a county have nabbed fake KSh 1,000 currency notes worth KSh 909,000 and arrested three suspects in two separate incidents.

The suspects were apprehended within Kandara constituency, with the first one identified as Stephen Murigi nabbed at Kabati Police Station after fake notes amounting to KSh 892,000 were recovered from the boot of his car.

Murang'a county police commander, Josephat Kinyua who spoke to TUKO.co.ke on Tuesday, July 16, said Murigi had been summoned to the police station by the officer commanding police station (OCS) over a matter he was being investigated for.

He said it was after a random check of his vehicle that the officer recovered the fake money.

"The officers also recovered a plate machine used to prepare the notes plus an ink solution normally spread to make the fake note impressions," Kinyua disclosed.

A source who spoke to TUKO.co.ke on condition of anonymity said members of the public had in the days leading to his arrest become suspicious of Murigi whose vehicle was almost always parked at one spot in Kabati town for long hours.

In the second incident, Joyce Muthoni and Raphael Njoroge were arrested at Kaburugi trading centre in Ng'araria ward within the same constituency after presenting fake KSh 17,000 that they had requested an M-Pesa operator to credit into their mobile phone accounts.

The suspects were cornered by an irate mob after the trader discovered that the notes were not genuine and raised an alarm.

"The trader who they found at the M-Pesa outlet tricked them he was not the one who attends to customers and urged them to wait so he could go and inform the owner of the shop who was in another room adjoining the outlet only for him to raise an alarm. Members of the public who responded blocked their vehicle and handed them to police officers at nearby Kaburugi Administration Police camp," the source disclosed.

Story by Mark Wachira, Tuko.co.ke correspondent, Murang'a county.
