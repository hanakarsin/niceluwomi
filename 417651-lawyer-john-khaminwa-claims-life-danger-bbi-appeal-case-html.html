Senior Counsel John Khaminwa Claims His Life Is in Danger after Hearing of BBI Appeal

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Wednesday, April 24, 2024
  • Two men claiming to be Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission (EACC) detectives allegedly stormed John Khaminwa's office on Saturday
  • This came a day after his submissions at the Court of Appeal against the Building Bridges Initiative appeal case
  • The lawyer termed the incident as intimidation since the appeal had drawn interest from different people across the world

Celebrated lawyer John Khaminwa now claims his life is in danger a day after his submissions at the Court of Appeal against the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) appeal.

In the Friday, July 2, session, the senior counsel urged the seven-judge bench presiding over the petition to dismiss President Uhuru Kenyatta’s appeal.

Speaking on Saturday, July 3, Khaminwa alleged that advocates representing clients against the BBI appeal case were being frustrated and intimidated.

This comes after two men claiming to be Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission (EACC) detectives allegedly stormed his office on Saturday afternoon.

The lawyer termed the incident as intimidation since the case had drawn interest from different people across the world.

Consequently, Khaminwa called on the Inspector-General of Police to ensure all the lawyers who participated in the BBI appeal are given security.

EACC denies raid

On the other hand, the anti-corruption agency chief executive Twalib Mbarak denied reports that detectives from the commission were behind the raid at Khaminwa's offices.

"EACC wishes to notify the public that it did not conduct any operation yesterday and media reports suggesting that EACC visited the offices of John Khaminwa Senior Counsel are unfounded," said EACC.

Khaminwa, appearing for the Kenya Human Rights Commission and Kituo Cha Sheria, argued that Uhuru had shown disrespect to judges and courts.

''My Lord, it is my humble submission that when you are coming before a court, you cannot despise a judge, and at the same time, you appear before that judge and expect that judge to give you orders at all,'' Khaminwa said.''When you have a president who is not obeying court orders when you have a president who is demeaning the judges before the public, this court must stand firm and say to him, no, we shall not hear you, we shall not give you orders at all,'' he added.
